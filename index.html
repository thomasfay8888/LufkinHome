Warning Signs

Does Your Mobile Home Need Leveling?

East Texas soil shifts constantly — especially after heavy rain or drought. When the ground moves, your mobile home moves with it. Here are the most common warning signs that your home needs professional releveling.

🚪

Doors That Stick or Won't Close

When your frame shifts even slightly, doors and windows bind up. This is one of the earliest and most obvious signs of settling.

📐

Uneven or Sloping Floors

If floors feel like you're walking uphill, or furniture tips, the piers underneath have settled unevenly. Don't ignore it — it gets worse.

🔍

Cracks in Walls or Ceilings

Cracks along drywall seams, around window frames, or in paneling are your home's way of telling you the structure is under stress.

🛁

Plumbing or Drain Problems

Pipes run at angles under your floor. When the home shifts, drains back up and water lines can crack. Leveling often fixes "plumbing" problems.

❄️

Drafts & High Energy Bills

An unlevel home creates gaps in skirting and wall joints, letting cold air in and conditioned air out. Your AC and heat run harder than they should.

🏗️

Soft or Spongy Floors

If floors feel soft, bouncy, or are starting to sink in spots, piers may have shifted or floor joists may have moisture damage — both are fixable.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the questions we get asked most often about mobile home leveling in Lufkin and East Texas.

How much does mobile home leveling cost in Lufkin, TX?
Most single-wide mobile home leveling jobs in the Lufkin area run between $300 and $800, depending on how many piers need attention and the condition of the blocking. Double-wide homes typically range from $600 to $1,500. We give you an exact price after seeing your home — always before any work begins.
How long does mobile home leveling take?
A typical single-wide releveling takes 3 to 5 hours. A double-wide usually takes a full day. In most cases, your home is fully usable the same day we finish — there's no waiting period or curing time required.
How often should a mobile home be leveled?
In East Texas, where we get heavy rainfall and seasonal soil movement, we recommend having your home inspected every 2–3 years. If you notice any of the warning signs — sticking doors, uneven floors, wall cracks — you should get it checked sooner. Early leveling prevents much more expensive structural repairs down the road.
Do I need to leave the home while you work?
No. You can stay in your home during the leveling process. Our crew works underneath the home. You may notice some movement in floors and hear some noise, but it's completely safe to remain inside. Many of our customers work from home while we level.
Will leveling fix my sticking doors and cracked walls?
In most cases, yes. Sticking doors almost always fix themselves once the home is back in frame. Wall cracks that resulted from settling will stop spreading immediately — though you may need to patch the cosmetic damage separately. We'll tell you honestly what leveling will and won't fix before we start.
Do you work on older mobile homes?
Absolutely. We work on all manufactured homes — 1970s single-wides, 1990s double-wides, and newer HUD-code homes. Older homes may need more extensive pier work, and we'll walk you through all of that during the on-site inspection. No surprise charges ever.
Do you serve areas outside Lufkin?
Yes — we serve all of Angelina County and most of East Texas, including Nacogdoches, Diboll, Jasper, Livingston, Center, San Augustine, and beyond. If you're within about 60 miles of Lufkin, we can get to you. Call us and we'll confirm your address is in our service area.
